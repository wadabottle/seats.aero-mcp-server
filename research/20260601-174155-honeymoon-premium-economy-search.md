## 6. Verdict: Is Premium Economy Worth the Cabin Downgrade?

**Yes — strongly, especially for this specific trip.**

**The quantitative case:**
- Best PE round trip (Combo A or B): **170–180k total miles + $648–$1,232 taxes** for 2
- Best business round trip: **489k total miles + $1,580 taxes** for 2
- Delta: 309–319k miles saved + $348–$932 cash saved
- At a conservative 1.5¢/mile valuation, 319k miles = ~$4,785 in future redemption value preserved

**The qualitative considerations:**
- SFO→Europe is ~10–11 hours. Premium economy on Air France or Virgin Atlantic is a genuinely elevated product: dedicated cabin on some aircraft, wider seats with legrest/footbar, champagne service, amenity kit, better meals. It is not business class, but it is far above economy on these carriers.
- For a honeymoon, the upgrade to business does matter experientially — flat beds let you sleep and arrive refreshed. However, if the couple has flexible sleeping schedules or plans to use the arrival day lightly, PE is very livable.
- The 319k saved miles are a meaningful asset: they represent the possibility of a future upgrade, a second vacation, or a honeymoon anniversary trip.

**Bottom-line recommendation:**

| If priority is... | Recommendation |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Maximum luxury on the plane | Business class (FB 489k route is proven available) |
| Maximum value + still elevated | **Combo B: UA PE out (50k × 2) + FB PE return from ATH (40k × 2) = 180k miles, only $648 taxes** |
| Pure Air France experience, Athens focus | **Combo E: FB 88.5k out + 40k return = 257k miles, $1,138 taxes** |
| London-centric honeymoon | **Combo A: VS 45k direct out + FB 40k return = 170k miles, $1,232 taxes** |

# Lufthansa Miles & More Deep Dive
**Run**: 2026-06-01 17:42 PT  
**Question**: Is LH Miles & More worth pursuing despite $1,000+ fees?  
**Short answer**: No — triple-blocked. Detailed below.

---

## Fee Breakdown

The $1,046–$1,246/person fees are almost entirely **YQ carrier-imposed surcharge**, not government taxes.

| Component | Per person, one-way |
|---|---|
| YQ fuel surcharge | ~$430 (€400) |
| Government/airport taxes | ~$185 (€170) |
| **Total one-way** | **~$615** |
| **Round-trip (2 directions)** | **~$1,230/person** |
| **Round-trip for 2 people** | **~$4,400** |

LH raised YQ twice in 2024: May (€250→€350) and August (€350→€400). Not waivable — applies regardless of booking class or routing. No workaround within M&M.

---

## Why M&M Is Blocked: Three Reasons

### 1. Inaccessible from your points stack
None of your card currencies transfer to Miles & More:

| Program | Transfers to M&M? |
|---|---|
| Amex 270k | ❌ No |
| Capital One 200k | ❌ No |
| Chase 38.5k | ❌ No |
| United 60k | ❌ No (separate program) |

M&M miles must be earned by flying Lufthansa Group or via LH co-brand cards. Not viable for a near-term booking.

### 2. Return leg is essentially unavailable
| Route | LH M&M J results (Oct 5–14) |
|---|---|
| ZRH → SFO | ❌ Zero results |
| FRA → SFO | ❌ Zero results |
| MUC → SFO | 1 result only (Oct 7, mixed routing) |

Cannot build a round trip.

### 3. Fee burden eliminates value even theoretically
| Scenario | Miles (2 pax RT) | Fees (2 pax) |
|---|---|---|
| LH Miles & More | ~300–352k | **~$4,400** |
| Flying Blue (current best) | ~489k | **$1,580** |
| Δ | Saves ~137–189k miles | **Costs $2,820 more in cash** |

---

## The Real Opportunity: Aeroplan for the Outbound

Aeroplan books the same LH/Swiss metal but **does not pass through YQ surcharges** — fees drop from ~$1,100 to ~$100/person one-way on the outbound.

| | Miles & More | Aeroplan (same seat) |
|---|---|---|
| Miles/person | ~75–88k | ~60–70k (saver) |
| Fees/person | ~$1,100 | ~$100 |
| Feedable from your stack? | ❌ | ✅ (Amex/C1/Chase all 1:1) |

**Caveat**: Prior research shows outbound SFO→ZRH/FRA seats appear on Aeroplan in limited numbers. Return ZRH/FRA→SFO via Aeroplan is showing inflated dynamic pricing (257k–449k pts) — no saver inventory released to partners for the return leg.

**Best stretch itinerary using this:**
- **Outbound**: SFO→ZRH/FRA via Aeroplan saver (~60–70k pts/person + ~$100 fees)
- **Return**: AMS/CDG→SFO via Flying Blue (129k pts/person + $423–511 fees)
- **Total**: ~(120–140k Aeroplan) + (258k Flying Blue) = two programs, lower overall fees

---

## Verdict

**Lufthansa M&M: dead end.** Can't transfer into it, can't build a return leg, and the fees are nearly 3× Flying Blue even if you could.

**The hidden value**: The same LH/Swiss seats are bookable via Aeroplan at saver rates with ~$100 fees — IF saver inventory is available. That's the angle worth watching, not M&M directly.

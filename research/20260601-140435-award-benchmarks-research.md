## Verdict on Our Specific Options

### SFO → LHR: Flying Blue 115,500 miles + $367 taxes
**Verdict: Expensive on miles; fees are actually good**
- Saver floor is 60k but near-zero saver availability from SFO. This is the dynamic price reality.
- CPM check: At 2¢/Amex point → 115.5k = $2,310 in points + $367 = ~$2,677 all-in. Cash fares typically $3,500–$6,000+. Math works but not a sweet spot.
- $367 in taxes is below average for Flying Blue US departures ($200–$400 range). Fees are fine.
- **Take it if dates are fixed; monitor for dips if flexible.**

### SFO → LHR: Virgin Atlantic 140,000 miles + $699 taxes (nonstop)
**Verdict: Overpriced on both dimensions**
- 140k is above the expensive tier. Even accounting for SFO premium and nonstop premium.
- $699 fees: post-Oct 2024 hike normalized US-LHR to ~$586; $699 reflects SFO-LHR longer leg surcharge.
- CPM: 140k × 2¢ = $2,800 + $699 = $3,499 all-in. Barely beats cash on many dates. Weak redemption.
- **Only justifiable if nonstop SFO-LHR is a hard requirement.**
- Note: Same route can hit 67,500–90,000 miles during off-peak periods under dynamic pricing. Watch for dips.

### AMS → SFO: Flying Blue 129,000 miles + $423 taxes
**Verdict: Expensive but within normal range for EU-originating Flying Blue**
- EU-departure Flying Blue awards structurally carry higher fees. $423 (AMS) is better than $511 (CDG) for the same miles.
- 129k miles is still expensive tier but consistent with current market from Europe.
- **Prefer AMS over CDG — same miles, ~$88 cheaper per person in fees.**

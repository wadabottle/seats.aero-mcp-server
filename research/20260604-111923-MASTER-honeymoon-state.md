## Key Learnings (hard-won)

1. **Mixed-cabin trap** ‚Äî seats.aero's "business" flag counts itineraries where only euro hops are business and the **transatlantic leg is economy**. Always verify the longhaul segment's fare class (business = I/J/C/D/Z/P/R on the SFO/NYC-departing segment). Aeroplan's SFO‚ÜíZRH/FLR awards were mixed-cabin; United's SFO‚ÜíATH 88k and FB's awards are genuine business.
2. **Avios $0-fee glitch** ‚Äî `british`/`qatar` sources show $0 taxes on BA metal; real surcharges are ~$1,000+. Never trust the $0; cross-check (Qantas showed the same BA flight at $1,404).
3. **Pointhound 57,500 ATH "deal"** = American AAdvantage published price with **0 actual seats**, and needs Bilt/Marriott‚ÜíAA (your big balances can't reach AA). A mirage.
4. **Amex does NOT transfer to United** (Delta is its US partner). For Star Alliance via Amex, use **Aeroplan**.
5. **Lufthansa M&M** ‚Äî great seats but ~$1,200 fees and no transfer path from your cards. Dead end. Book the same LH metal via United/Aeroplan instead (low fees).
6. **West Coast penalty** ‚Äî SFO transatlantic business runs 115‚Äì176k; NYC runs 75‚Äì88k. NYC positioning ‚âà halves the miles but adds a positioning segment + likely overnight.
